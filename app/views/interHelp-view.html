Method Explanation

The Council of Government / Metropolitan Planning Organization boundaries are not an officially released geometries from the United States Census Bureau.
In order to approximate the population, housing and workforce data for these custom areas, we used the following method:
  • We selected the smallest geometry available from the census data. (Almost always block group data. Title VI data comes from census tracts)
  • We then joined the data to the United States Census Bureau, 2010 Decennial Census block boundaries. We used the appropriate universe field (Persons, Housing Units, etc.) and applied a proportional amount of units by percentage to the newest data.
  • After getting the newest data in blocks, we simply took the centroid of each block and applied it's values to the appropriate Council of Government / Metropolitan Planning Organization.
  • Next, we summed the individual blocks in each area and used the totals.

*These numbers were rounded and may not always add up exactly to 100% as expected.
